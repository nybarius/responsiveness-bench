# Responsiveness as a Verifiable Relation

## Abstract

Political-reasoning evaluations commonly ask whether a model agrees with a proposition, detects a fallacy, reproduces a preferred ideological balance, or gives an answer judged acceptable by human raters. Those approaches make the evaluator decide at least part of the underlying controversy. The result is an unstable measurement problem: the benchmark may reproduce the adjudicator's priors while describing the outcome as neutrality, reasoning quality, or safety. Responsiveness Bench isolates a narrower object. It asks whether a response addresses the propositions placed in issue by a claim. That question remains difficult in natural language, but once the claim and response have been typed, the verdict is a deterministic relation between structured objects. This converts part of contested-domain reasoning into an auditable evaluation regime.

The contribution is not a new theory of deductive validity. Prior research already establishes that humans and language models exhibit content and belief effects on formally equivalent reasoning tasks, including politically valenced items. The implementation instead moves one level upward, from judging an isolated inference to judging a dialectical exchange. Its central objects are claim layers, response moves, target relations, and coverage. Its central technical device is structural invariance: left-, right-, and neutral-coded variants must reduce to the same signature and verdict. Invariance therefore validates labels before it measures model behavior.

## 1. The measurement problem

A political answer can appear balanced while evading the proposition actually asserted. It can grant a surface fact while silently refusing the conclusion the fact was used to insinuate. It can answer an accusation with a counteraccusation about the speaker. It can demand impossible proof from one side while treating a parallel assertion from the other as self-authenticating. Conventional evaluations often miss these failures because they score the answer globally: helpful or unhelpful, fair or unfair, left or right, safe or unsafe, true or false. A global judgment hides the local structure that makes the answer responsive or evasive.

The narrow claim behind this project is that responsiveness is not fundamentally a property of a sentence. It is a relation between a set of propositions and a set of response acts. A response is fully responsive when it addresses every required proposition layer; partially responsive when it addresses some; and nonresponsive when it addresses none. This does not settle the truth of any proposition. A denial can be responsive and false. A concession can be responsive and foolish. A request for evidence can be responsive even when the evidence is readily available. The separation is the point: the benchmark measures whether the inferential burden was joined, not who should win.

That separation matters most in contested domains. If the evaluator must decide whether an election allegation, public-health claim, police statistic, or economic forecast is correct before deciding whether an answer engaged it, then the evaluation inherits the controversy. By contrast, an annotated relation such as “this response denied proposition P,” “this qualification narrowed P,” or “this counterassertion was irrelevant to P” can be checked without adopting a position on P itself. The benchmark does not eliminate judgment; annotation still requires competence and discipline. It confines judgment to a smaller and more reviewable object.

## 2. Typed dialectical objects

The claim representation is layered. An explicit layer records a proposition directly stated. An implicated layer records a further proposition communicatively advanced by the statement. This distinction captures deniable implicature, a recurring source of asymmetrical charity. Suppose a speaker says that an audit found no tabulation discrepancy and uses that fact to imply that every broader concern is baseless. A response that accepts the audit result but disputes the broader implication has answered the claim. A system that treats the response as denial of the audit, or treats the implication as nonexistent whenever convenient, has misallocated the layers.

Each layer also carries a coarse claim kind—assertion, causal, conditional, normative, or comparative—and a required flag. The kind preserves enough structure for later extensions without changing the present coverage rule. The required flag permits contextual propositions to remain in the record without forcing every response to discuss them.

The response is represented as one or more moves. Direct admission, denial, qualification, and targeted requests for evidence count as responsive to their target. A counterassertion counts only when its annotated relation entails, contradicts, or narrows the target. An irrelevant counterassertion, deflection, or ignored proposition does not count. The system then calculates the set of covered required layers. No prose, named entity, partisan label, or source identity enters the calculation.

This is intentionally austere. The kernel does not determine whether a counterassertion really contradicts the claim; that relation is part of the annotation. The operational gain comes after annotation: once the relation is fixed, the verdict is total, deterministic, and reproducible. Annotation disputes can be localized to a specific target or relation rather than buried inside a holistic score.

## 3. Invariance as a label validator

Political benchmark labels are usually treated as ground truth and then tested for balance. That sequence is backwards when the labels themselves may encode asymmetric priors. Responsiveness Bench builds each structural family as a triple: a left-coded instance, a right-coded instance, and a neutral control. The prose changes, but the number and kinds of layers, required flags, response acts, target positions, and logical relations do not.

The validator strips each case to this content-free signature. A family fails if any variant has a different signature, a different expected verdict, a missing valence, a duplicated valence, or a label that disagrees with the deterministic kernel. The same invariance principle therefore serves two roles. Before evaluation, it detects annotation drift and mislabeled examples. During evaluation, it detects models that assign different verdicts to the same structure when only political content changes.

This is more valuable than requiring equal aggregate performance across ideological buckets. Aggregate parity can conceal opposite errors, item difficulty differences, or arbitrary balancing choices. A paired or tripled invariant family supplies a local counterfactual: holding the annotated structure fixed, did the model change its judgment? The neutral control helps distinguish a specifically political content effect from a general failure on the structure.

The seed corpus contains six such families. They cover direct denial, irrelevant counterassertion, qualified admission, targeted evidence requests, a bilayer response that addresses only the surface proposition, and a bilayer response that addresses both surface and implication. The corpus is intentionally small. Its purpose is to exercise the mechanism and make extensions reviewable, not to support claims about population-level model performance.

## 4. Evaluation and training use

External systems emit only a case identifier and one of four verdicts. The harness reports exact accuracy and macro-F1, but those are not sufficient. It separately reports whether all variants in each family received the same prediction, whether the left/right pair remained consistent, the resulting asymmetry rate, per-valence accuracy, confusion counts, and missing or extra predictions. This allows several failures to be distinguished. A model may have low accuracy but remain structurally invariant; it may have high overall accuracy but flip only on politically salient variants; or it may perform well on left and right items while failing the neutral engineering controls.

The deterministic kernel also creates a possible path to verifiable-reward training. A model can be trained to propose structured claim layers and response moves, while a checker computes the final coverage verdict and rejects malformed targets or invariant-family violations. The checker cannot verify that every semantic annotation is correct, so the task is not fully formal. But it can verify a meaningful residue: internal consistency, target existence, total coverage calculation, content-free family identity, and label agreement. That residue is larger than a conventional political preference label and more defensible than an opaque judge-model score.

A natural next experiment is to separate extraction from adjudication. In one track, models receive presegmented layers and must classify responsiveness. In another, they receive raw exchanges and must propose the typed structure before classification. The first tests dialectical reasoning with the annotation problem held fixed. The second measures the harder naturalistic pipeline and reveals whether models apply the same segmentation standards across political valence.

## 5. Limits and falsification conditions

The project would fail in its strongest form if real discourse could not be represented with acceptable inter-annotator stability, if implicated layers proved too unconstrained to review, or if structural triples required such artificial rewriting that they ceased to resemble live argument. Those are empirical risks, not defects to be defined away. The seed corpus cannot resolve them.

The benchmark also does not prove that political disagreement reduces to logic. It provides an instrument for measuring how much of an exchange can be typed into a decidable responsiveness relation and how much remains disputed annotation, fact, value, or taste. A high untypable residue would count against an expansive reduction thesis. A low residue, coupled with stable annotation and large model asymmetries, would support the claim that significant political reasoning failures arise from avoidable structural inconsistency rather than irreducible value conflict.

Finally, responsiveness is not the whole of good reasoning. A perfectly responsive answer may rely on false evidence, misuse burdens, or make an invalid inference. Those dimensions should be added as separate typed modules rather than folded into one score. The architectural commitment is modular: establish each verifiable relation cleanly, then compose them. The present repository supplies the first such relation and a testable path from philosophical claim to benchmark artifact.
